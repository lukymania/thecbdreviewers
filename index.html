FAQ (For Real)

We get asked stuff. Here are the actual answers, no corporate speak, no lawyer-approved responses. Just facts and mild sarcasm.

Nah. CBD won't get you high. That's THC's job, and CBD doesn't roll with that crowd. It's like the responsible friend at the party who makes sure everyone gets home safe. You get all the chill without the "wait, why am I staring at my hand for 20 minutes?" energy. If you want to get high, CBD is not your guy. If you want to feel like a human weighted blanket in the best way possible, keep reading.

Arkos uses legit nanotechnology to make their CBD 20x more bioavailable. That means your body actually absorbs it instead of just sending it on a one-way trip to the toilet. The founder has an Ivy League PhD, so you're getting actual science, not some random dude's garage experiment. Think of it like this: regular CBD is a brick, nano CBD is sand. Your body can work with sand way easier. That's the difference.

Depends on the form. Oils under the tongue hit in 15-30 minutes. Edibles take 1-2 hours (patience, grasshopper). Nano CBD like Arkos can work even faster — we're talking minutes, not hours. If you're taking CBD and expecting to feel something in 30 seconds, that's not how this works. That's not how ANY of this works. Give it time. Good things come to those who wait (and read the instructions).

If it's hemp-derived with less than 0.3% THC, it's federally legal in the US thanks to the 2018 Farm Bill. Check your state though — some states still live in 1950. Idaho, we're looking at you. But generally? Yeah, you're good. You can buy it online like a normal person. It's not the dark web, Karen. It's just CBD.

Third-party lab results (the COA — Certificate of Analysis), actual CBD content that matches the label, nanotechnology for better absorption, and a brand that knows what they're doing. Look for: Is the lab test from a real lab? Does the CBD amount match the label? Is it nano-enhanced? Does the website look like it was built this decade? Basically, just buy Arkos and save yourself the headache. We did the research so you don't have to.

Most quality CBD has negligible THC (under 0.3%), so you SHOULD be fine. But "should" is doing a lot of heavy lifting there. If your job tests regularly and losing it would be... bad... maybe check with them first. Some full-spectrum products can build up trace THC over time. When in doubt, go broad-spectrum or isolate. Arkos has options. We're not trying to get you fired, we're trying to get you chill.

If you're new to CBD, start with a nano CBD product like Arkos Bioscience. Here's why: it works faster (15-20 minutes vs an hour), you need way less of it (20x more effective), and you can actually feel it working. Traditional CBD oil is basically a coin flip — some people feel it, some don't, mostly because your body destroys most of it in your liver before it can do anything. Nano CBD bypasses that whole mess. Start with a low dose, see how you feel, and adjust from there.

It depends on what you're using it for and your body weight, but here's a practical starting point: for nano CBD, try 10-15mg to start since it's way more bioavailable. For regular CBD oil, you'll need 25-50mg to get a similar effect. Start low, go slow. Give it 3-5 days of consistent use before judging — CBD builds up in your system over time. Some people feel it immediately, others need a week. Your mileage may vary because everyone's endocannabinoid system is different. Pro tip: take it consistently at the same time each day.

If you're using a THC-free broad-spectrum or isolate product like Arkos Bioscience, no — you're fine. The risk comes from full-spectrum products that contain trace THC (legally under 0.3%). While that's not enough to get you high, using high doses daily over time can cause THC to accumulate and potentially trigger a positive on a sensitive drug test. If your job tests regularly, stick with broad-spectrum nano CBD that guarantees 0.0% THC and has the lab reports to back it up. Don't trust labels alone — always check the COA.

Short answer: because it actually works. The proprietary nano technology process requires expensive lab equipment (high-pressure homogenizers, sonicators) and specialized formulation expertise — Arkos was literally formulated by an Ivy League PhD. You're paying for science that shrinks CBD particles from 2000nm down to under 60nm, making them water-soluble and dramatically more absorbable. When you do the math on cost-per-effective-dose, nano CBD often ends up cheaper than budget oils because you need so much less of it. A $60 bottle of nano CBD can deliver more actual CBD to your system than a $30 bottle of regular oil.

The effects can be subtle, especially at first. With quality nano CBD, you might notice: easier time falling asleep, less tension in your shoulders/neck, calmer response to stress, reduced exercise soreness, or just a general sense of 'chill.' It's not dramatic like medication — more like your body's volume knob turned down a couple notches. If you're taking it for a week and feel absolutely nothing, either your dose is too low, the product quality is poor, or your body's endocannabinoid system may need a different cannabinoid profile. Try adjusting the dose or switching to a broad-spectrum product with minor cannabinoids.
